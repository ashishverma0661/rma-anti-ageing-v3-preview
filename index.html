Anti-Ageing & Rejuvenation Treatments

Regenerative aesthetics designed around skin biology — medically led, evidence-based, and tailored to natural-looking outcomes.

Ageing skin is not simply a cosmetic concern — it is a biological process driven by declining collagen, elastin, and hyaluronic acid production. Over time, skin gradually loses firmness, hydration, elasticity, and structural support, leading to fine lines, laxity, dullness, and a fatigued appearance.

At Restart Medical & Aesthetics Clinic, our focus is not on overfilling or creating artificial results. Instead, we use regenerative aesthetic treatments that work with your skin’s natural repair systems to stimulate collagen production, improve skin quality, and restore healthier-looking skin over time.

Understanding Skin Ageing

Visible ageing is mainly influenced by three biological changes. Effective plans usually combine several complementary approaches rather than relying on a single procedure.

01Driver

Collagen & Elastin Decline

From the mid-twenties onward, collagen production steadily decreases. Elastin — responsible for skin recoil and flexibility — also weakens over time, leading to sagging, thinning skin and reduced firmness.

~1% / year from age 25
02Driver

Reduced Skin Hydration

Natural hyaluronic acid levels decrease with age, causing dehydration, dullness, and increased visibility of fine lines — even before they're etched in.

~50% drop by age 50
03Driver

Repetitive Facial Movement

Years of facial expression eventually carve deeper static lines around the forehead, eyes, and mouth. Skin's ability to recover from each fold reduces with age.

Glabella · crow's feet

Safety, Aftercare & Long-Term Maintenance

All treatments are performed using strict clinical hygiene and safety protocols. Aftercare guidance is provided in writing following every appointment.

Temporary side effects

Most settle within a few days and are limited to the injection site:

  • Mild redness & tenderness
  • Localised swelling
  • Occasional bruising
  • Small papules at injection points

For 24–48 hours, avoid

Standard aftercare for most regenerative protocols:

  • Excessive heat (sauna, sun)
  • Alcohol
  • Intense exercise
  • Active skincare ingredients

Best long-term results

Regenerative aesthetics work best when combined with:

  • Consistent medical-grade skincare
  • Daily sun protection
  • Healthy lifestyle habits
  • Regular maintenance reviews

What we don't do

Honesty over upselling. We will not treat where it isn't clinically appropriate:

  • Pregnancy or breastfeeding
  • Active skin infection at site
  • Conditions contraindicating the treatment
  • Cases better suited to surgery or dermatology

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about anti-ageing and regenerative treatments.

What is the difference between filler and regenerative treatments?

Fillers restore structure and volume, while regenerative treatments improve skin quality by stimulating collagen and tissue repair. They sit alongside each other in a treatment plan rather than being interchangeable.

How long do results take?

Anti-wrinkle injections work relatively quickly — visible from around day five with peak effect at two weeks. Regenerative treatments improve gradually over several weeks as collagen remodelling develops.

Will the results look natural?

Yes. Our treatments are designed to create subtle, refreshed, natural-looking improvements. We deliberately avoid the over-treated, frozen or overfilled look.

Is PRP safe?

PRP uses your own blood, making it naturally compatible and widely considered low-risk when performed clinically. It is drug-free and hypoallergenic by definition.

Can treatments be combined?

Yes. Many patients combine anti-wrinkle injections with regenerative treatments such as Profhilo, polynucleotides or PRP for more comprehensive rejuvenation.

Will I need maintenance treatments?

Maintenance treatments help preserve results, but stopping treatment will not accelerate the ageing process beyond its natural course. Your skin returns to its untreated trajectory — it doesn't decline faster than it would have otherwise.
